Insight #1: Average Rating¶

The first thing I want to look at is what is the average rating?

I'm normalising the values by diving by the denominator and then multiplying again by 10 to deal with the few values that have a very high numerator and high denominator.

In [4]:
analysisDf['normRatings'] = ((analysisDf.rating_numerator / analysisDf.rating_denominator)*10)
In [5]:
print('Mean:', analysisDf.normRatings.mean())
print('Median:', analysisDf.normRatings.median())
print('Mode:', analysisDf.normRatings.mode())

The average value is 12/10 but interestingly the median is lower at 11/10. This suggests the distribution is skewed I'm interested to plot what it is.

The high value was for a very high rating of 1776/10 given to one dog that is skewing the mean. I could remove that value but don't want to seeing as it's a real rating.

One way to avoid this skewing in the plot would be to take the median instead of the mean. This will be less skewed by high values.

In [13]:
medianRatingsWithTime = analysisDf.normRatings.groupby([analysisDf.timestamp.dt.year, analysisDf.timestamp.dt.month]).median()

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
medianRatingsWithTime.plot()
plt.title('Median Ratings with Time')
plt.xlabel('Date (Year, month number)')
plt.ylabel('Median Rating Out of 10')
plt.show()

This makes the increase with time even clearer. It's now also blockier as median value each month is a whole number.

You can really see the walk from 10 to 13.
